Unique Geological Structures

An arachnoid is a large geological structure resembling a spider web.

Complex Appearance and Origin

They are of unknown origin, and have been found only on the surface of the planet Venus. They appear as concentric ovals surrounded by a complex network of fractures, and can span 200 kilometers. One possible explanation of its origin is that an upwelling of magma from the interior of the planet pushed up on the surface, causing cracks. The arachnoid group of Bereghinya Planitia is by far the largest arachnoid concentration on Venus.
