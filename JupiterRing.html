Jupiter's Mysterious Ring System:

A Cosmic Dance of Dust and Gravel



Overview

Jupiter has a bleak planetary ring system, about 6,500 kilometers wide and less than 10 kilometers thick.

Composition and Characteristics

It consists of a large amount of dust and black gravel, rotating around Jupiter with a cycle of about 7 hours.

Three Main Components

The ring consists of three main parts: the inner side is like a recepta, a halo ring composed of particles, a relatively bright main ring in the middle, and a tulle ring in the outer ring. ( These rings seem to be composed of dust, unlike Saturn's rings are composed of ice.)
