Triton:

Neptune's Icy Moon Adventure



Neptune's Unique Moon

Triton is the largest moon of Neptune and was the first to be discovered. It’s the only large moon in the solar system that circles its planet in a direction opposite to the planet's rotation, which suggests that it may once have been an independent object that Neptune captured.

Extremely Cold and Geological Activity

Triton is extremely cold, with surface temperatures around minus 391 degrees Fahrenheit. It’s one of the few moons in the solar system known to be geologically active.

Composition and Atmosphere

Triton has a surface of mostly frozen nitrogen, a mostly water-ice crust, an icy mantle and a substantial core of rock and metal. Active geysers are discovered to erupt sublimated nitrogen gas to the air, giving Triton a thin nitrogen atmosphere less than 1⁄70,000 the pressure of Earth's atmosphere at sea level.
