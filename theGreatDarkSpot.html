The Great Dark Spot on Neptune

The Great Dark Spot (also known as GDS-89, for Great Dark Spot, 1989) was one of a series of dark spots on Neptune. In 1989, GDS-89 was the first Great Dark Spot on Neptune to be observed by NASA's Voyager 2 space probe.

Characteristics of Great Dark Spots

Great Dark Spots are anticyclonic storms, with a high-pressure center. Their lifetimes appear to be relatively short, forming and dissipating once every few years or so. The knowledge about its origins, movement, and disappearance is little.

Impressive Size and Shifting Nature

The Great Dark Spot is as wide as Earth and has shown the ability to shift north-south over time.
